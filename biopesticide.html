A biopesticide is a type of pesticide derived from natural sources such as bacteria, fungi, viruses, plants, or minerals. Unlike chemical pesticides, biopesticides control pests in a more sustainable and eco-friendly way.

They work through natural mechanisms like predation, parasitism, or microbial competition to manage insects, weeds, fungi, and harmful bacteria.

üåø Types of Biopesticides

  • Microbial Biopesticides: Based on microorganisms like bacteria or fungi. Example: Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt).
  • Botanical Biopesticides: Derived from plants. Example: Neem oil, pyrethrin.
  • Biochemical Biopesticides: Natural substances that affect pest behavior. Example: Insect pheromones.

üåé Benefits of Biopesticides

  • Environmentally Friendly: Safer for people, animals, and the planet.
  • Target-Specific: Less harmful to non-target species and beneficial insects.
  • Biodegradable: Break down quickly without leaving toxic residues.
  • Resistance Management: Reduce the risk of pest resistance buildup.

  • Short Shelf Life: Tend to degrade faster than synthetic pesticides.
  • Slower Action: May not kill pests immediately.
  • Environmental Sensitivity: Effectiveness can vary with weather conditions.
  • Requires Knowledge: Proper application is key to success.

üå± The Role of Biopesticides in Sustainable Agriculture

Biopesticides are essential for Integrated Pest Management (IPM) and organic farming. As the demand for safer, more sustainable agriculture grows, biopesticides are becoming a preferred choice for both farmers and consumers.
