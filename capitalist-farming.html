Capitalist farming is an agricultural system focused on treating farming as a business enterprise. It emphasizes profit maximization through large-scale production, advanced technology, and market orientation.

üìã Key Characteristics of Capitalist Farming

  • Private Ownership: Farms owned by individuals or corporations.
  • Profit-Oriented: Main goal is financial gain.
  • Large Scale Operations: Extensive use of land and machinery.
  • Use of Technology: Heavy mechanization and biotech use.
  • Wage Labor: Employs hired workers instead of family labor.
  • Market Dependence: Production guided by demand and prices.

üåæ Types of Capitalist Farming

  • Commercial Grain Farming: Large-scale cereal production.
  • Industrial Livestock Farming: Intensive meat, dairy, poultry production.
  • Plantation Farming: Export crops like coffee and cotton.
  • Dairy Farming: Business-oriented milk production.
