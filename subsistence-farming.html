Subsistence farming is a form of agriculture focused on growing just enough food to feed the farmer's family or community. It is characterized by small landholdings, traditional methods, and reliance on family labor rather than commercial production.

  • Small-scale farms: Usually only a few acres.
  • Family labor: Most work done by family members.
  • Diverse crops: Growing various crops for balanced nutrition.
  • Minimal technology: Limited use of machinery or chemicals.
  • Low surplus: Little or no extra produce for sale.
  • Traditional practices: Use of local knowledge and age-old methods.

üåø Common Crops and Livestock

  • Crops: Maize, rice, wheat, millet, tubers, vegetables.
  • Livestock: Chickens, goats, sheep, cattle in small numbers.
  • Mixed farming: Combining crop and animal husbandry.

üåç Importance of Subsistence Farming

  • Provides essential food security for millions.
  • Preserves cultural heritage and traditional farming methods.
  • Promotes environmental sustainability through natural practices.
  • Supports rural livelihoods and local economies.

  • Low productivity due to limited inputs and technology.
  • Vulnerability to climate change, pests, and diseases.
  • Land pressure from population growth.
  • Difficulty accessing markets and fair prices.
  • Risk of poverty and economic instability.
