Organic farming is a method of agriculture that emphasizes natural processes, biodiversity, and soil health. Unlike conventional farming, organic farming avoids the use of synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, GMOs, and chemical additives. Instead, it relies on ecological balance and sustainable techniques to grow crops and raise animals.

At its core, organic farming is about working with nature, rather than trying to control it. It‚Äôs a holistic approach to agriculture that focuses on long-term sustainability and the health of the environment, farmers, and consumers.

  • Soil Health: Enhancing soil fertility through composting, crop rotation, and green manure.
  • Biodiversity: Promoting a variety of plants and animals to support ecosystem health.
  • Natural Inputs: Using natural fertilizers and pest control methods like neem oil or beneficial insects.
  • Animal Welfare: Raising animals in humane, natural conditions with organic feed.

  • Sustainability: Supports farming without depleting resources.
  • Better for Farmers: Encourages safer working conditions.
